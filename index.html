02 / Bio

Bio

Nejma Nefertiti is a Brooklyn-based Hip Hop artist and modern-day alchemist whose work moves fluidly across sound, scent, fashion, and code. At the root of it all is the music: fierce delivery, multisyllabic rhyme, and vivid, cinematic imagery that has earned her international recognition.

As a songwriter, recording engineer, and executive producer, she has been seen and heard on Shade 45's Rap Is Outta Control, Sway In The Morning, Sway's Universe, Chuck D's "And You Don't Stop," and the Rock The Bells platform, and was one of 12 winners of The Saga Continues Contest, organized by Sway and RZA of Wu-Tang Clan. She has graced stages including BAM, The Kennedy Center, SOB's, The Nuyorican Poets Cafe, The Knitting Factory, The Blue Note Jazz Club, The Apache Cafe, and The Phoenix Concert Theatre, and blessed audiences across France, Switzerland, Brussels, Canada, and Serbia.

Her catalog runs deep: a discography of albums, EPs, and singles, along with hundreds of features and collaborations, sound design and film scoring, and a growing list of sync placements, the record of a true working artist. She directs the visual world of her projects with the same ferocity she brings to every verse, shaping the artwork and imagery that carry her music. Not only is she a lyricist and wordsmith, but for the last ten years, she's been a teaching artist, building curriculum that puts Hip Hop in service of Black, Brown, and Indigenous communities, equipping the next generation with tools for authorship, ownership, and creative agency. Her voice has carried from the Mesopotamian Water Forum in Iraq to the First Ecosocialist International in Venezuela, speaking and performing on Hip Hop as a vehicle for solidarity and cultural preservation.
